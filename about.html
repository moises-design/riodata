The Story

Why we built this.

The Laredo–Brownsville corridor handles more trade than most countries — but nobody knew who the local contractors were.

When billion-dollar energy and infrastructure projects started arriving in South Texas, the developers had one problem: they couldn't find qualified local businesses fast enough. The region had the workforce and the companies — there was just no way to connect them.

At the same time, local businesses had no platform to showcase their certifications, capacity, and readiness. A HUBZone-certified welder in Laredo had the same visibility as someone with no certifications — essentially none.

RioData was built to fix that. A free, verified directory of regional businesses. A live project opportunity board. A workforce intelligence layer. All of it free, forever — because this is infrastructure, not a product.

The goal is simple: make South Texas and Northern Mexico as legible to investors, developers, and institutions as any other major American metro.

Our Principles

How we operate.

🆓
Free Forever for Businesses
No fees, no subscriptions, no paywalls for the businesses and workers this platform is built to serve. Revenue comes from institutional data licensing — never from the region's small businesses.
✅
Verified, Not Scraped
Every company is submitted directly and reviewed before going live. We'd rather have 50 verified businesses than 5,000 unverified listings. Quality over quantity, always.
🌎
Both Sides of the Border
This region doesn't stop at the Rio Grande. Our coverage spans South Texas and Northern Mexico — Laredo to Brownsville, Nuevo Laredo to Monterrey to Matamoros.
📊
Data as Public Infrastructure
Economic data shouldn't be locked behind expensive subscriptions. Regional intelligence should be available to every business, researcher, and institution that needs it.
🤝
Built With the Region
This platform is built by people from this region, for this region. We're not a Silicon Valley company that discovered the border — we're from here.
🚀
Infrastructure, Not a Product
RioData is designed to be acquired by or licensed to the institutions — universities, EDCs, banks — that serve this region long-term. We're building the foundation, not a startup.
